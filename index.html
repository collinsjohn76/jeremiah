Jeremiah Collins
Musician · Drummer · Company G, 150th N.Y. Infantry

From the small town of Dover in Dutchess County, New York, Jeremiah enlisted as a 15-year-old boy and emerged three years later a seasoned veteran who had marched from Poughkeepsie to Savannah and back. He wrote these reminiscences in 1903 — nearly forty years after the war — for the committee preparing the regiment's official history.

The 150th New York Infantry — "The Dutchess County Regiment"

Organized entirely from Dutchess County, New York, and led by Colonel John H. Ketcham of Dover, this regiment was regarded as unusually cohesive — many of its men were neighbors, friends, and relatives who had grown up together. It mustered in on October 10, 1862 and was mustered out June 8, 1865 — serving the full duration of the war.

About this document: Jeremiah Collins wrote these pages in 1903 for the "Committee on Revision" overseeing the preparation of a regimental history of the 150th New York — eventually published in 1907 as The Dutchess County Regiment. Jeremiah noted he never kept a diary, so he wrote entirely from memory forty years after the fact. These reminiscences were made available by his great-grandson, Vincent A. Powell, and have been shared here with additional historical research for context and accuracy. The post-war photograph of Jeremiah appears on the final page of his original manuscript.
